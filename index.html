About Me


Hello! I'm an ex-MOE physics tutor who has taught in schools like RGS and SNGS.

Students like my lessons for my energy and how I make abstract concepts relatable, digestible and hence enjoyable!
I believe constructing knowledge and developing skills are highly personal, so I make the effort to connect with every student, diagnose their unique challenges, and journey together, no matter how low things might be.

The Problem


#1: Mass-produced, fast-food education.
There are many good schools and tuition centres out there, but class sizes can often be overloaded. Even in large tuition centres, lessons are hence pre-decided, catering only to a perceived majority.
Physics deals with math, language, visualisation and application skills. Hence, many end up falling behind based on their different weaknesses. Introverts especially may not get the most out of the lesson. Without sufficient attention and personalisation, many latent issues in learning can go undiscovered.

#2: Myopic and superficial focus on grades.
Many education places also focus on delivering content and exam grades. While understandable, this may not produce transferrable success (e.g. from 1 subject to another) and long-term success in other areas of life (e.g. in university, jobs and in society).
Instead, a good time investment would be in developing important thinking skills, especially metacognitive skills. This will naturally boost study and exam skills anyways, and often with greater success.

# Finding a Solution #
I believe in the effectiveness of small group learning. Hence, I tutor classes of up to 3 max, unless students form larger class with friends. This allows me to better interact with and engage each child, especially those introverted like me!
I also believe in a medical consultative approach, and have used it to much success and positive feedback. Grades are but a diagnosis of some deeper symptoms, such as poor study habits, inefficient study methodology, difficulties in focusing, or struggling to be motivated and on-task. These symptoms in turn have root causes, such as not addressing perfectionism, attention deficiency, neurodivergence, personality triats that hinder learning, even neglecting of general well-being (especially sleep!), and more.
Over the years, while I did help many in Physics, it was more exciting to go beyond to the roots. With my qualifications in pedagogy, strong interest in psychology, years of experience and passion to help students succeed not just in Physics but in life, I have:
  • helped students understand themselves better.
  • have partnered with students and parents identify and tackle mitigating factors from the ground up.
  • guided students to become better at studying.
  • even helped one start their journey to recognising neurodivergence and eventually clinically diagnosing ADHD.
I am excited to continue helping more, so join me if you're looking for this help for your child or for yourself!
